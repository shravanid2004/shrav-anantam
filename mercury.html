Mercury : The swift innermost planet..

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun, orbiting at an average distance of about 57.9 million kilometers (36 million miles). This small, rocky planet has a surface characterized by craters, similar to the Moon, due to the lack of a significant atmosphere to protect it from meteoroid impacts. Temperatures on Mercury are extreme, ranging from scorching highs of 430°C (800°F) during the day to frigid lows of -180°C (-290°F) at night, making it one of the most inhospitable places in the solar system. Despite its proximity to the Sun, Mercury does not have a significant atmosphere; instead, it has a very thin exosphere composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium. Its lack of moons and geological activity adds to its unique status among the planets.
