Jupiter: The gas giant with a swirling storm.

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, known for its massive size, thick atmosphere, and iconic Great Red Spot—a gigantic storm that has been raging for centuries. This gas giant is composed mostly of hydrogen and helium and lacks a well-defined solid surface. Jupiter's powerful magnetic field and numerous moons, including the four largest known as the Galilean moons (Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto), make it a fascinating object of study. Io is the most volcanically active body in the solar system, while Europa is believed to have a subsurface ocean that may harbor conditions suitable for life. Jupiter's immense size exerts a strong gravitational influence on surrounding bodies, playing a significant role in shaping the solar system's architecture.
