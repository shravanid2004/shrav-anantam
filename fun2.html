MIND WOBBLING

Gamma-Ray Bursts (GRBs) Gamma-ray bursts are the most energetic explosions observed in the universe, typically associated with supernovae or the collision of neutron stars. A GRB can release more energy in a few seconds than the Sun will emit over its entire lifetime. If a GRB were to occur close enough to Earth (within a few thousand light-years), the intense radiation could strip away the Earth's ozone layer, exposing the surface to harmful ultraviolet radiation. This would result in mass extinction events, devastating ecosystems and human life.
