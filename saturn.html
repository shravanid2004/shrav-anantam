Saturn: The ringed beauty of the solar system.

Saturn is renowned for its stunning rings, which are made up of ice particles, rocky debris, and dust. This gas giant is the second-largest planet in the solar system and is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, similar to Jupiter. Saturn has a diverse atmosphere featuring storms and high-speed winds, and its low density means it could float on water if a sufficiently large body existed. Saturn has a rich collection of moons, with Titan being the largest; it possesses a thick atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane and ethane, making it a prime candidate for astrobiological studies. The intricate ring system extends thousands of kilometers into space, composed of thousands of individual ringlets, and changes over time due to gravitational interactions and collisional events.
